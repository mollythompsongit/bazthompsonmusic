About the artist

Baz Thompson is a singer/songwriter/multi-instrumentalist from Wellington, New Zealand. Formerly a frontman for Lower Hutt's legendary band Lemon Spitfire, and latterly as the drummer for the celebrated rockers The Stacks, he since went off-grid and produced three full-length solo albums--2015's debut "Anonymous Superstars," 2020's follow-up "Into the Dark," and the recently released "Crossfire Saturday."
Chock full of real-life experiences, and more abstract, ethereal themes, his music encapsulates moments of intense retrospection, through to sardonic bursts of irony, intermingled with smatterings of humour. The music behind the words that drive the songs into the listeners' ears ranges from tender melodies to avalanches of crashing drums and guitars, and as all the instrumentation is from a single source the individual performances are never competing with each other for prominence. He tracks, mixes and masters his own work, creating a distinctly unique sound and musical style.

Background: Heavily influenced by the post-punk new wave artists from the mid-eighties, the cacophony of nineties alt-indie-grunge, and the more contemporary synth-soaked pop, Baz's songs convey these themes with his own individual twist. Music you can judge for yourself, he has enjoyed creating whilst simultaneously agonised over.
